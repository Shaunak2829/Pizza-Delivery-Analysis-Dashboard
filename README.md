# Pizza-Delivery-Analysis-Dashboard

# 🍕 Pizza Delivery Sales & Operations Analysis | Power BI Dashboard

## Short Description

An end-to-end Power BI dashboard analyzing **1,084 pizza delivery orders** across **6 restaurant brands** and **10 US cities**, covering sales performance, revenue trends, payment behavior, and delivery efficiency.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Power BI Desktop** | Dashboard development & visualization |
| **Power Query Editor** | Data cleaning & transformation |
| **DAX** | KPI measures & calculated columns |
| **`.pbix`** | Development file format |
| **`.png`** | Dashboard preview exports |

---

## 📂 Data Source

- **Platform:** [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/)
- **Scope:** Multi-year pizza delivery data (2024–2026) across US cities covering orders, revenue, payments, and delivery operations

---

## ✨ Key Findings & Highlights

### 📊 Sales Overview
- Total Revenue: **$44,105** | Orders: **1,084** | Avg Order Value: **$40.70** | Avg Rating: **3.69/5**
- Orders dropped sharply from **443 (2024) → 268 (2026)** — a ~40% decline, indicating a retention problem
- **May peaks at $7.4K** while July drops to just **$1.6K** — strong seasonality to act on
- **Atlanta leads revenue at $3.2K**; bottom cities like Boston and Chicago sit at **$1.8K**
- Card and Apple Pay together drive **~48% of total revenue** ($21K combined)

### 🚚 Delivery Performance
- High traffic adds **~10 extra minutes** — avg time goes from **23.6 min (Low)** to **34.2 min (High traffic)**
- Delay rates hit **60% at lunch (11AM–1PM)** and **50% at dinner (8–9PM)** — the two biggest bottlenecks
- **Domino's has the highest delay rate at 40%**; Little Caesars is best at **34%**

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- **Investigate the order drop** — loyalty programs or re-engagement campaigns needed urgently
- **Add riders during 11AM–1PM and 8–9PM** to cut delay rates in peak windows
- **Run July promotions** to recover an estimated **$2,000–$3,000** in lost seasonal revenue
- **Audit Domino's operations** — its 40% delay rate is the highest across all brands
